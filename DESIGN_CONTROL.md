# MonticellOPS Design Control — Revision 2

## Purpose
MonticellOPS guides progressive reduction of uncertainty through disciplined teamwork. It is not a project tracker.

## Fixed roles
- Mission Commander: Nicole
- Recon Ops: Chris
- MonticellOPS: the source of operational knowledge

## Operating model
Mission Brief → Objective → Commander Guidance → Recon Execution → Evidence Check → Capability Established → Next Objective → Mission Debrief

## Interaction requirements
- Calm, dialogue-based language; not terse military role-play.
- Retain the terms Mission Commander, Recon Ops, Mission, Objective, Reconnaissance, Capability Established, and Mission Debrief.
- The app tells the Commander where to send Recon Ops, which device to use, how to position it, what to capture, and what proves completion.
- Recon Ops should almost never need outside research or ask “How do I do this?”
- Every objective provides purpose, location, method, X/Y/Z reference, setup, execution, evidence, and completion criteria.
- Never represent approximate field references as surveyed control.

## Coordinate convention
Objective 1 establishes Reference A at the northeast corner of Lot 2, where the property line with Lance meets the county-owned Monticello Drive buffer.
- X=0, Y=0, Z=0 at Reference A
- +X runs west into Lot 2
- +Y runs south
- Z means height above local ground unless a surveyed elevation is explicitly loaded

## Progress and debrief
Progress emphasizes Current Objective and Next Objective, not productivity statistics. Completion unlocks capabilities. Debrief summarizes uncertainty reduced, capabilities established, unresolved constraints, and whether the mission felt calm and self-explanatory.


## Communication standard added in Revision 3
The app addresses the Mission Commander as a capable adult who may not have prior engineering vocabulary. Prefer familiar professional language, explain unfamiliar concepts where they become useful, connect instructions to purpose, and preserve technical rigor in the underlying requirements.
